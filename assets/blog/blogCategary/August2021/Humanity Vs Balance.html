Humanity Vs Balance...

By

Team Sankalp
Reputed builder
August 27, 2021


Where it all started:-

I was lying on my back, looking at the dark sky, thinking how much I can wrap the vast universe with my little eyes. Then it hit me that even if the universe is this enormous, There must be a reason why the earth is the only habitable planet in our solar system and the only planet where life is prospering.

Then I remember that one episode of discovery in which they said The earth is in a goldilocks zone, also known as the habitable zone, meaning we are not too close and not too far from the sun for liquid water to exist. Call it the great balance if you will. If this balance had disturbed even in the slightest, life wouldn't have existed at all.

The part of our lives:-

Nature is both antagonist and protagonist. When Nature is at its finest is comparable to the heavens but when it shows you its worst side, the destruction is unimaginable.

Balance exists in the smallest or the most significant possible thing you can imagine.

Even in our daily life, balance is the most crucial part. We are experiencing the first wave of monsoon. If you live in urban areas, you might have a different opinion than me, but Nature is at its best behaviour in the countryside. Where I live, the mountains colour themselves green, and Grass sways as rivers sing melodies.

The role we played:-

I can't blame Nature Because we are the ones leeching off of it at our convenience. Nature is most beautiful when it's undisturbed and we don't appreciate it as much as we should and Our only contribution is overpopulation, pollution, burning fuels, and deforestation, which results in climate change, soil erosion, poor air quality, and polluted water.

We only amass the benefits from it and never give them back. Even if we forget, Nature is all-knowing, So it returns a thousandfold in the form of global warming, Floods, etc. That's how Nature balances itself.

The new normal:-

We can't ignore the pandemic many of our friends and family members had left us. The aftermath of this pandemic is horrible. We are indeed living in the worst time in the 21st century. You might relate to this if you are living in Mumbai. Some are cooped up in our closed space, waiting for this lockdown to be over. People are moving to the countryside not only because they are shut-in but for their safety also.


Even though you feel caged, you can't stay away from your workplace, so you need to accommodate your work-life balance. Find the no lockdown areas nearby where you can enjoy the family weekend and blow off some steam.

Therefore nature is the birthplace of memories and an irreplaceable home for humanity.
